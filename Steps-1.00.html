Problem Statement:

Lung cancer is the third most common cancer in the US with 1 in 16 people will be diagnosed in their lifetime. Between 2003 and 2013 the number of people in the US that are diagnosed with lung cancer increased by 6.21%. Study also shows close to 65% of all new cases of lung cancer are among people who have never smoked or are former smokers.

Although more lives are lost to lung cancer than any other cancer, only 6% of federal government dollar spent on cancer research are spent on lung cancer research. The main purpose of this project is to create a dashboard that helps health professionals and policy makers to monitor populations most affected by lung cancer, evaluate the success of programs designed to prevent cancer and diagnose cancer at early stages.
